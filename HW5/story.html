My first choir audition

My first choir audition was during my Senior year of high school where I auditioned for county choir. The whole process of it was fun, because I spent the whole month practicing for something that I wanted to achieve. However, it was really unfortunate come the time of my audition, because I had lost my voice the day of the audition.

In a panic I tried to do everything I could to get my voice well. Some of the things that I tried were drinking as much water as I could, Downing 5 canisters of ginger tea, eating ginger candy, and not speaking until my audition. However, I was unable to get my voice back by the time the audition came.

Thus afterwards I went to the warmup room where I warmed up by doing some scales, however my voice was struggling to do really trivial scales. After warming up for a little bit I went with my friends to the area where our audition rooms were, and waited for my turn. When it was my turn I went in and did my audition. Surprisingly it was not terrible, and I was happy with my performance. I then went to do the second half of my audition which was for tonal memory, and I think I did amazing in that section, because I was in the flow of performing.

Finally when the scores had come back I learned that I was only 6 points away from the cutoff, and in the end I was happy with how I did. While I might have done better if I had my voice that day, I do not think I did the worst job in the world, and did way better than I could have imagined.
